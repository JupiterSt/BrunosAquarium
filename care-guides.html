Acclimation (Fish, Corals, Inverts)

  • Turn off lights to reduce stress. Dim room if possible.
  • Float sealed bag 15–20 min to equalize temperature.
  • Open bag; add ~1/4 cup tank water every 5 min for 20–30 min (or drip at 2–4 drops/sec for 30–60 min).
  • Net livestock into tank. Discard bag water (do not pour into tank).
  • Lights low for first few hours; feed lightly next day.

Tip: Have Prime/conditioner and an aerated bucket handy. Sensitive inverts may need slower drip times.

Cycling a New Tank

The nitrogen cycle establishes beneficial bacteria that convert ammonia → nitrite → nitrate. Most setups take 2–6 weeks depending on seeding and temperature.

Basic Steps

  • Add dechlorinated water and start filtration/heater.
  • Add a bacteria starter and a small ammonia source (fish food or bottled ammonium per directions).
  • Test every 2–3 days: ammonia, nitrite, nitrate.
  • When ammonia & nitrite are zero and nitrate is present, perform a water change and begin stocking slowly.

Helpful Gear

  • Reliable test kit (shop kits)
  • Biological media & flow
  • Temperature control (76–80°F most systems)

Saltwater (Fish‑only)

Temp 74–78°F • pH 6.8–7.6 • Ammonia/Nitrite 0 • Nitrate < 40 ppm (species dependent)

Reef Lighting Basics

Place corals according to their light demand. Aim for stable photoperiods (8–10 hours) and adequate flow.

  • Soft corals: ~50–100 PAR
  • LPS corals: ~75–150 PAR
  • SPS corals: ~200–350 PAR

Start low and increase slowly to avoid bleaching. Ask us to help place new frags.

Feeding

Fish

  • Mix frozen, pellets, and nori for variety.
  • Small portions 1–2× daily; remove uneaten food.

Corals & Filter Feeders

  • Target feed appropriate particle size.
  • Use live foods when possible (pods & phyto).

Compatibility & Stocking

  • Research temperament; avoid mixing known aggressors.
  • Quarantine when possible; add most peaceful fish first, territorial species last.
  • Follow tank size guidelines—upgrade plans matter.

Unsure about a pairing? Ask us before you buy—we love planning safe stocking lists.

Quarantine Tips

  • Simple 10–20g tank with heater, sponge filter, and cover.
  • Observe 2–4 weeks; feed well and monitor behavior.
  • Keep separate tools to avoid cross‑contamination.
